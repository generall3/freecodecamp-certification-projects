Timeline

  • Feb 1955 - Steve Jobs was born on 24th of February 1955 in San Francisco
  • 1960 - The Jobs family moves from San Francisco to Mountain View, a suburban town in Santa Clara county, more commonly known as Silicon Valley
  • 1969 - Steve Jobs meets Steve Wozniak, 5 year older, through a mutual friend. Woz and Steve share a love of electronics
  • 1972 - Steve Jobs build and illegally sell 'blue boxes' that allow to make phone calls for free
  • Dec 1973 - In december 1973, Steve Jobs attend but later drops out of Reed College. He starts to travel through India until 1974. During his time in India, Steve becomes more interested in Buddhism
  • Mar 1976 - Woz and Steve show the early Apple I board at the Homebrew Computer Club
  • Apr 1976 - Apple Computer Inc. is incorporated by Steve Jobs, Wozniak and Ron Wayne
  • 1978 - The Apple II becomes the first mass-market personal computer, with impressive sales around the US. Apple becomes a symbol of the personal computing revolution
  • Sep 1985 - Steve resigns from Apple and starts NeXT with five other refugees from Apple. Apple announces it will sue NeXT
  • Jan 1992 - Next licenses its operating system, NeXTSTEP, to run on x86 machines
  • Dec 1996 - Apple, which was desperately looking for a modern operating system to buy, eventually buys NeXT for $400 million. Steve is named "informal adviser" to Apple CEO Gil Amelio
  • July 1997 - Gil Amelio is ousted by the Apple Board of directors after a disastrous quarter. Steve is named interim CEO in his place and installs his NeXT executive team at the top of Apple
  • Oct 2001 - First iPod is introduces and Apple opens retail stores
  • May 2005 - Steve gives commencement speech at Stanford University. In the same year Steve unveils iWork, the Mac mini, iPod shuffle and Mac OS X 10.4
  • June 2007 - Introduction iPhone, Apple TV and iPod Touch
  • Jan 2010 - Jobs introduces iPad, iPhone 4 and iPod Touch 4G and new Macbook Air
  • Aug 2011 - Steve Jobs resigns as CEO of Apple, with the words 'I have always said if there ever came a day when I could no longer meet my duties and expectations as Apple's CEO, I would be the first to let you know. Unfortunately, that day has come'. Tim Cook becomes Apple CEO
